Coding can open doors for you

Is ‘learn to code’ on your list of goals this year?

Perhaps you teach in a school or your child is learning, you work for a large company or run a small business. Maybe you’re in transition or you have an app idea. Learning to code can empower you.

Code is a huge part of our daily lives: it powers your phone, your bank account, your social networks... it’s everywhere.

We think it’s essential to understand how code works and to make it work for us.

Learning to code will broaden your skills and make you indispensable in the workplace. You’ll begin to understand the endless possibilities of technology. You’ll have the knowledge to get ideas off the ground and communicate seamlessly with developers (and budding child prodigy programmers).

People everywhere are cottoning on to this and learning to create on the Web. Even 10 year olds... This is definitely a good thing :)

There’s a course for that!

New to coding? We know it’s tough sifting through the endless resources with no-one to direct all your questions. Learning with others is fun and much easier than hacking away at online courses alone.

We’re here to make it simple.

Get started on your learning journey mastering basic HTML, CSS and Javascript. You’ll also learn the foundations of good design and start prototyping your ideas.

Join us on our 6 week saturday course at Ravensbourne. You don’t need any prior experience. Discuss, learn and make with fellow determined individuals in a friendly, relaxed environment.

You’ll have access to laptops and lovely experts with plenty of links and resources to take away.

Is this course for me?

Teachers

We know a few teachers who feel daunted and unsupported by the new (now year old) computing curriculum.

With a solid knowledge-base and clear vision of how to build on it, you’ll look forward to, and even enjoy, coding with your students.

Parents

You could be the cutting-edge parent who codes with their kids for fun :)

Once you grasp the basics of coding, you’ll be able to get creative with your children, building cool stuff together and supplementing the things they learn at school.

Up-skillers

Perhaps you’re looking for ways to make yourself more valuable in the workplace, or 2016 is the year you’re going to move to a new position, company or industry.

Boosting your tech skills will give you a competitive edge and open up new directions in your career.

In transition

Investing in yourself and your skills is a great way to make some positive life changes for 2016. You may want to explore your career options or take a leap outside of your comfort zone.

In our personal experience, learning to create with code is hugely empowering and can give you a real confidence boost. You’ll be surprised at just what you can achieve!

What's the plan?

  • Get to know each other
  • Discuss: what is an app, how do we use them, how do we start building one?
  • Introduction to HTML and CSS: create your app home screen

01

  • We work together to generate, spec out and test your ideas
  • User Experience research: interview people (aka users)
  • Get to know JavaScript: connect your app to an online database

02

  • Interface design: sketch out your app’s pages
  • Learn how to use digital wireframing tools
  • Mark up your wireframes with HTML and CSS

03

  • Review your wireframes collaboratively
  • JavaScript: learn about variables, functions and loops
  • Code your app with JavaScript

04

  • Make!
  • Test!
  • Tweak! Repeat...

05

  • Finalise and present your app prototype
  • Celebrate your achievements!
  • Take away resources to continue on your app-making journey

06
